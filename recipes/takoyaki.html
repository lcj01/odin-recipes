Takoyaki

Description

Takoyaki, or Grilled Octopus Balls, are one of Japan's best-known street food that originated in Osaka. Whether you make a traditional style with bits of octopus or other alternatives, these ball-shaped dumplings are fun to make with your friends and family!

Ingredients

  • 5 ounces cooked octopus
  • katsuobushi flakes
  • 1 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon kombucha
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 teapsoon soy sauce
  • 1 2/3 cup dashi stock
  • 1/2 cup chopped green onions
  • 1/3 cup tenkasu
  • takoyaki sauce
  • spicy mayo

Directions

  1. Combine dry ingredients. This includes: 1 cup of all purpose flour, 1 teaspoon of kombucha, and 1 tsp baking powder
  2. Whisk together 2 eggs and 1 teaspoon of soy sauce
  3. Add the dashi stock slowly, a little at a time
  4. Heat the takoyaki pan on medium high heat
  5. Wait until you see smoke rising from the pan before adding the batter
  6. Add the octopus, green onion, tenkatsu and powdered katsuobushi to the takoyaki
  7. Set a three minute timer
  8. Break the connected batter between each bowl after the timer goes off
  9. Set a timer for 4 minutes. Constantly turn the balls
  10. Transfer takoyaki to a plate and add sauce and spicy mayo
